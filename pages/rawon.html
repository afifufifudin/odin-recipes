Rawon: Indonesia's Nutty, Black Beef Symphony

Ingredients

For the Keluak Paste:

  • 5 keluak nuts (black nuts), roasted, peeled, and mashed (wear gloves when handling!)
  • 4 shallots, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, chopped
  • 1 inch ginger, chopped
  • 1/2 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1/4 tsp coriander seeds
  • 1/4 tsp black peppercorns
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil

For the broth:

  • 500g beef chuck or brisket, cut into cubes
  • 1.5L water
  • 2 lemongrass stalks, white parts only, bruised
  • 3 kaffir lime leaves
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup tamarind paste

For the oppings:

  • 150g green beans, blanched
  • 100g bean sprouts
  • 1 hard-boiled egg, halved (optional)
  • Fried shallots
  • Chopped cilantro
  • Sambal terasi (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Make the Keluak Paste: Blend shallots, garlic, ginger, spices, and keluak nuts into a smooth paste. Fry in oil until fragrant and slightly browned.
  2. Simmer the Broth: In a large pot, combine beef, water, spices, salt, brown sugar, and tamarind paste. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 1.5 hours, or until beef is tender. Add the keluak paste and simmer for another 30 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  3. Assemble the Soup: Add blanched green beans and simmer for 5 minutes. Remove beef and set aside.
  4. Serve: Divide broth and beans among bowls. Top with sliced beef, halved egg (optional), bean sprouts, fried shallots, and cilantro. Drizzle with sambal terasi (optional) for extra intensity.

Tips

  • If fresh keluak nuts are unavailable, you can use store-bought keluak paste for convenience.
  • For a lighter broth, you can skip the keluak paste altogether.
  • Leftover rawon tastes even better the next day! Store it in the fridge for up to 3 days.

Dive into the dark depths of rawon and unlock a universe of flavor. Its unique blend of earthy, savory, and slightly sweet notes will leave you wanting more. So, gather your ingredients, embrace the adventure, and experience the magic of Indonesian cuisine!
