Pecel: A Symphony of Vegetables in Peanut Sauce

Ingredients

For the Peanut Sauce:

  • 150g raw peanuts
  • 50g brown sugar
  • 3 red chilies (adjust for spice preference)
  • 2 shallots, roughly chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, roughly chopped
  • 1 kaffir lime leaf, torn
  • 1/2 inch ginger, roughly chopped
  • 1/4 cup tamarind paste
  • 1 cup water
  • Salt to taste

For the Salad:

  • 250g water spinach (kangkung), blanched
  • 150g green beans, blanched
  • 100g bean sprouts, fresh
  • 1 cucumber, thinly sliced
  • 1 hard-boiled egg (optional)
  • Sambal terasi (optional, for extra oomph)

Instructions:

  1. Make the Peanut Sauce: Toast the peanuts in a dry pan until golden brown and fragrant. Grind them into a coarse powder in a spice grinder or mortar and pestle.
  2. In a pan, heat oil and fry the shallots, garlic, and chilies until fragrant. Add the ginger and kaffir lime leaf, cook for another minute.
  3. Add the ground peanuts, brown sugar, tamarind paste, and water. Bring to a simmer and cook until the sauce thickens and flavors meld. Season with salt to taste.
  4. Assemble the Salad: Arrange the blanched vegetables and cucumber on a plate. Drizzle with the peanut sauce and toss gently. Top with a sliced hard-boiled egg and a dollop of sambal terasi (optional).
  5. Enjoy! Dig in and savor the harmonious blend of textures and flavors. The crunchy vegetables, creamy peanut sauce, and a hint of spice make this a truly unforgettable dish.

Tips

  • Feel free to customize your salad with other vegetables like cabbage, carrot, or papaya leaves.
  • For a richer flavor, you can toast the peanuts and spices in coconut oil.
  • Leftover pecel tastes even better the next day! Store it in the fridge for up to 2 days.
